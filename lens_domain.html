Domain Lens

Description: The conceptual model of the problem space. Core domain concepts, aggregates, bounded contexts, ubiquitous language, invariants.

General Level Guidance

Level 1 — Introduction

  • What is the 'Core Domain' vision? What primary business problem is this domain solving?
  • What are the high-level subdomains (Core, Supporting, Generic)?
  • What is the high-level 'story' or narrative of how this domain creates value?

Common Fulfillment: A Domain Vision Statement and a high-level subdomain categorization.

Level 2 — Orientation

  • What is the core vocabulary (Ubiquitous Language) used across the domain?
  • How is the domain divided into Bounded Contexts?
  • What are the conceptual relationships (Upstream/Downstream) between these contexts?

Common Fulfillment: A DDD Context Map and a Glossary of core business terms.

Level 3 — Elaboration

  • What are the specific Entities, Value Objects, and Aggregates within a context?
  • What are the hard business invariants that must never be broken?
  • How does the state of a domain object change over its lifecycle (State Machine)?

Common Fulfillment: Aggregate diagrams, State Machine diagrams, or links to the 'Domain' folder in the source code.

Example: Completely over-engineered Todo-App

Level 1 — Introduction

The domain vision is to transform fragmented human intent into organized, actionable progress. We focus on the 'Core Domain' of productivity management, while delegating 'Identity' and 'Communication' to generic supporting subdomains.

Level 2 — Orientation

The domain is mapped via two primary Bounded Contexts. The Productivity Context handles the 'what' and 'when' of tasks, while the Identity & Access Context handles the 'who' and 'permission'.

Ubiquitous Language (Glossary)
  • Task: A single unit of actionable work with a deadline.
  • List: A logical grouping of tasks with a shared ownership model.
  • Backlog: A specific type of list containing tasks without immediate due dates.
  • Grace Period: The time window after a subscription expires where Pro features remain active.
Context Map Narrative

The Identity context is Upstream to the Productivity context, providing the security principal needed to authorize list access. The relationship is a 'Customer-Supplier' model where Productivity depends on the stability of the Identity schema.

Level 3 — Elaboration

Task Aggregate Structure

The Task is an entity within the TaskList Aggregate Root. A Task cannot exist outside of a TaskList. Modifications to a task's priority must be validated against the TaskList's global priority constraints to ensure no more than 3 'Critical' tasks exist per list.

Business Invariants & Rules
  • State Transitions: A task can only move from Pending to In-Progress if it has an assigned Owner.
  • Collaborator Limits: A standard list is limited to 5 collaborators; Pro lists have no limit. This is enforced at the domain service level.
  • Due Date Validation: Due Date must be in the future for newly created tasks, unless marked as Imported.
Lifecycle State Machine

The TaskLifecycle follows a complex state machine: Created -> [Pending | Blocked] -> In-Progress -> [Awaiting-Review | Completed] -> Archived. Each transition triggers domain events that are consumed by the Notification and Analytics services.
