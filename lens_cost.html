Cost Lens

Description: The economic perspective of the architecture. Covers infrastructure spend, licensing, operational costs, and environmental sustainability (GreenOps).

General Level Guidance

Level 1 — Introduction

  • What are the primary cost drivers for this architecture (e.g. Data Egress, Managed Services)?
  • What is the overall FinOps philosophy (e.g. "Reserve Instances by default", "Pay-as-you-go")?
  • What are the high-level budget constraints or ROI targets?

Common Fulfillment: A high-level Cost Strategy statement or a 'Top 5 Cost Drivers' list.

Level 2 — Orientation

  • How are costs allocated across teams or services (e.g. Tagging Strategy)?
  • Which licensing models (Open Source, SaaS, Enterprise) are in use?
  • What is the high-level infrastructure spend per month/year?

Common Fulfillment: Links to Cloud Billing consoles (e.g. AWS Cost Explorer) and the central Licensing Inventory.

Level 3 — Elaboration

  • What are the specific cost-optimization levers (e.g. Spot instances, Gravity-based routing)?
  • What is the carbon footprint or 'Green score' of specific technical choices?
  • How exactly does the system scale down to zero (or minimum) to save costs?

Common Fulfillment: Detailed unit-cost analysis per transaction, FinOps dashboard links, and Sustainability impact reports.

Example: Completely over-engineered Todo-App

Level 1 — Introduction

Our strategy is 'Optimize for Agility first, then for Scale'. We accept higher initial costs for managed services (EKS, RDS) to minimize operational staff overhead.

Level 2 — Orientation

Monthly spend is concentrated in EKS (40%), RDS (30%), and Data Transfer (15%). We use a strictly enforced AWS Tagging policy (Project, Environment, Owner) to automatically generate monthly cost-center reports for each engineering squad.

Level 3 — Elaboration

Cost Optimization Lever: Karpet-Autoscaler

We use Karpenter instead of the standard Cluster Autoscaler. Karpenter allows us to use AWS Spot Instances for 80% of our worker nodes, reducing compute costs by ~60%. Production workloads are 'weighted' to prefer Reserved Instances for the remaining 20%.

Sustainability (GreenOps)

To reduce our carbon footprint, we have implemented 'Nightly Shutdown' for the Development and Staging environments. These clusters scale down to zero replicas between 10 PM and 6 AM, saving approximately 33% of our non-production compute energy.
