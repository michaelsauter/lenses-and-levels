Capability Lens

Description: What the system enables the business to do. Describes business capabilities, value streams, feature groupings, and ownership domains. Bridges strategy and implementation.

General Level Guidance

Level 1 — Introduction

  • What are the high-level business goals this system supports?
  • What is the primary value proposition for the users?
  • What are the 3-5 'North Star' capabilities?

Common Fulfillment: A high-level Capability Map or Business Canvas.

Level 2 — Orientation

  • How are capabilities grouped into logical business domains or value streams?
  • Which technical components (from the System Lens) realize which business capabilities?
  • What are the primary dependencies or shared foundations between these capability groups?

Common Fulfillment: A Capability-to-System mapping matrix or a Value Stream Map.

Level 3 — Elaboration

  • What are the specific features and sub-capabilities within a domain?
  • How do capabilities depend on or block each other at a functional level?
  • What are the specific business KPIs or metrics used to measure the success of this capability?

Common Fulfillment: Link to Product Backlogs (Jira/Linear), Roadmap tools (Productboard), or analytics dashboards.

Example: Completely over-engineered Todo-App

Level 1 — Introduction

The platform provides digital Personal Productivity and Team Collaboration capabilities, enabling users to transition from individual task tracking to complex project management.

Level 2 — Orientation

Capabilities are structured into three primary business domains. The Core Productivity domain (CRUD, task organization) is realized primarily by the Todo Service. The Collaboration Hub domain (sharing, real-time presence) leverages both the User Service and the Sync Engine. Finally, the Platform Foundation provides cross-cutting capabilities like global search and audit trails.

Each capability maps to specific system containers; for example, the 'Smart Task Scheduling' capability is provided by the Todo Service interacting with a dedicated ML-inference sidecar, while 'Identity Management' is delegated to an external provider.

Level 3 — Elaboration

Collaboration Hub Decomposition

The collaboration capability is broken down into four granular sub-capabilities: Shared Lists, Task Delegation, Activity Streams, and Presence Management. Shared Lists require integration between the User Service (for RBAC) and the Todo Service (for data isolation).

Business Value Stream: Pro Onboarding

The onboarding stream is a cross-capability sequence designed to maximize conversion:

  1. Identity Verification (Identity Context).
  2. Sample Data Generation (Productivity Context).
  3. Collaborator Invitation Flow (Collaboration Context).
Capability Dependency Map

Presence Management has a hard runtime dependency on the Socket Sync Engine. Task Delegation depends on the Notification Service. If the Notification Service is down, Delegation remains 'Degraded' (tasks are assigned but users are not alerted).
