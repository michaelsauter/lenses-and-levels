Relation to Established Standards

The Lenses & Levels model is designed to be fully compatible with major architectural frameworks while providing a more modern, audience-driven structure. Below is a high-level mapping of all our lenses to established standards:

Lens arc42 Equivalent TOGAF / ISO 42010
System Lens Context & Building Blocks Application Architecture
Capability Lens Business Context Business Architecture (Capabilities)
Domain Lens Building Blocks (Domain Model) Information Architecture (Entities)
Data Lens Concepts (Data Model) Data Architecture
Integration Lens Interfaces (Building Blocks) System Integration Viewpoint
Runtime Lens Runtime View Technology Architecture (Dynamic)
Deployment Lens Deployment View Technology Architecture (Physical)
Security Lens Concepts (Security) Security Architecture
Quality Attributes Lens Quality Requirements Quality Viewpoint
Governance & Decision Lens Architectural Decisions Architecture Governance
Evolution Lens Infrastructure / Concept Transition Architecture
UX & Interaction Lens Concepts (UI/UX) Business Architecture (User View)
Organization Lens Context (Organizational) Business Architecture (Org Units)
Cost Lens Constraints Architecture Realization (Financial)
Compliance Lens Constraints / Risks Governance & Compliance

The 'Levels' in our model serve as a replacement for traditional 'Stakeholder Viewpoints' by providing the right degree of detail for the specific audience's needs.

Mapping Principles

The mapping above demonstrates how Lenses & Levels captures all traditional architectural concerns while organizing them by audience interest (Lens) rather than strictly by technical layer. This approach makes documentation more accessible to non-technical stakeholders while maintaining the rigor required by standards like ISO 42010.
