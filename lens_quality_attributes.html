Quality Attributes Lens

Description: How non-functional requirements are realized. Performance, availability, scalability, maintainability, observability, operability.

General Level Guidance

Level 1 — Introduction

  • Which specific 'Qualities of Service' (e.g. Availability, Scalability, Security) are the primary drivers for the architectural design?
  • What are the most critical runtime constraints or performance targets?
  • How do these priorities align with the business value proposition?

Common Fulfillment: A prioritized list of Quality Goals and a brief 'Why' statement for each.

Level 2 — Orientation

  • What is the overall strategy for measuring and alerting on these qualities?
  • Which high-impact scenarios (e.g. 'Complete Data Center Outage', '10x Traffic Spike') is the system designed to survive?
  • What are the fundamental architectural trade-offs (e.g. 'Latency vs. Consistency') made to achieve these goals?

Common Fulfillment: A set of high-level Quality Attribute Scenarios and links to documented trade-off analyses.

Level 3 — Elaboration

  • What are the precise Service Level Objectives (SLOs) and their associated indicators (SLIs)?
  • How is the observability stack configured to detect and root-cause quality violations?
  • What are the results and frequency of automated load, stress, or chaos testing?

Common Fulfillment: Link to live SLO dashboards (e.g. Grafana) and the latest automated performance test reports.

Example: Completely over-engineered Todo-App

Level 1 — Introduction

The system prioritizes 99.9% Global Availability and Sub-200ms Interactive Latency to provide a seamless productivity experience for power users.

Level 2 — Orientation

We achieve our availability targets through Multi-AZ redundancy at every tier: ALB, EKS Node Groups, and RDS multi-region replicas. Performance is monitored using a unified observability stack: Prometheus for metrics, AWS X-Ray for tracing, and Grafana for real-time visualization.

Scalability is handled via independent scaling of the 'Read Path' (RDS Read Replicas) and 'Write Path' (Kubernetes HPA). Our core measurement strategy uses Synthetic Transactions that simulate a user adding and completing a task every 60 seconds from five global locations.

Level 3 — Elaboration

Service Level Objectives (SLOs)
  • Availability: < 43 minutes of downtime per month.
  • Latency: 95% of GET /tasks requests must be served in < 150ms from the nearest edge.
  • Integrity: 0% data loss during zonal failure (RPO = 0).
Architectural Trade-offs (CAP Theorem)

To maintain high Availability and Partition Tolerance, we have chosen Eventual Consistency for task list counts across devices. A user might see a slightly stale count on their mobile device for ~2-3 seconds after adding a task on their laptop. This prevents 'blocking' the UI while waiting for global synchronization.

Observability Dashboard Schema

Our 'Golden Signal' dashboard tracks:

  1. Latency: Time taken to process task CRUD.
  2. Traffic: Requests per second per service.
  3. Errors: Rate of 5xx responses (Triggering automated rollback if > 1%).
  4. Saturation: EKS Node CPU/Memory pressure.
Load Testing Results (Q4 2025)

Stress testing showed the Todo Service saturates at 4,500 RPS per pod due to Node.js event loop lag. Our current HPA configuration is tuned to spawn new pods at 3,000 RPS to maintain head-room for sudden traffic spikes (e.g., morning login peaks).
