Welcome to The Lenses & Levels Architecture Documentation Model

This documentation framework proposes a novel approach to software architecture documentation, combining different "lenses" of architectural concerns with consistent "levels" of detail for each lens. This two-dimensional structure aims to provide clarity and relevance to various audiences, from newcomers to deep implementers.

The Core Idea:

Traditional architecture documentation often struggles with overwhelming detail or lack of specific focus. The "Lenses & Levels" framework addresses this by organizing documentation around two key axes:

  • Lenses: Each lens represents a distinct architectural concern, such as System, Capability, Data, Security, etc. This allows stakeholders to focus on the aspects most relevant to their interests.
  • Levels: For each lens, information is presented across three consistent levels of detail: Introduction, Orientation, and Elaboration. This ensures that the documentation scales with the audience's need for information, from a high-level overview to deep technical specifications.

Benefits:

  • Targeted Information: Readers can quickly find information relevant to their role and current need, without being bogged down by irrelevant details.
  • Consistent Structure: A predictable structure across all lenses makes the documentation easy to navigate and understand.
  • Scalability: Accommodates various levels of detail, from executive summaries to implementation-level specifics, and links naturally to living artifacts.
  • Comprehensiveness: Ensures all critical architectural concerns are covered through dedicated lenses.
