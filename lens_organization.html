Organization Lens

Description: How the human organization aligns with the technical architecture. Covers team topologies, ownership boundaries, and cognitive load management.

General Level Guidance

Level 1 — Introduction

  • What is the overall organizational structure (e.g. Squads, Tribes, Hierarchical)?
  • How does the organization align with Conway’s Law?
  • What are the high-level ownership philosophies (e.g. "You build it, you run it")?

Common Fulfillment: An Org Chart or a high-level Team Structure diagram.

Level 2 — Orientation

  • Which team is responsible for which technical components?
  • What are the different team types (e.g. Stream-aligned, Platform, Enabling)?
  • How do teams communicate and hand over work?

Common Fulfillment: A Team Topologies diagram or an Ownership Matrix (RACI).

Level 3 — Elaboration

  • What are the specific on-call and rotation procedures for each team?
  • How is cognitive load measured and mitigated for a specific stream-aligned team?
  • What are the specific interaction modes (e.g. Collaboration, X-as-a-Service) between teams?

Common Fulfillment: Link to Team Charters, On-call schedules (PagerDuty), and internal 'Ways of Working' documents.

Example: Completely over-engineered Todo-App

Level 1 — Introduction

We follow a decentralized squad model where engineering teams are aligned with specific business domains to maximize speed and autonomy, explicitly mirroring our microservices structure.

Level 2 — Orientation

Our teams are categorized using Team Topologies:

  • Squad 'Alpha' (Stream-aligned): Owns the Productivity Domain and Todo Service.
  • Squad 'Sigma' (Stream-aligned): Owns the Collaboration Domain and Sync Engine.
  • Core Platform (Platform Team): Owns the EKS Cluster, CI/CD pipelines, and shared libraries.

Level 3 — Elaboration

Team Interaction Modes

Squad 'Alpha' consumes the EKS cluster as X-as-a-Service from the Platform team. However, when Alpha needs a new infrastructure capability (e.g. a specific RDS extension), they enter a short-term Collaboration period with the Platform team to co-develop the Terraform module.

Cognitive Load Management

To prevent overload, Squad 'Alpha' is explicitly *not* responsible for the legacy Java monolith. That monolith is 'trapped' in a maintenance mode managed by a dedicated transition team, ensuring Alpha can focus 100% on the new Go/Node.js stack.
