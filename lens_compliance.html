Compliance Lens

Description: The regulatory and legal perspective. Covers industry standards (SOC2, HIPAA), data residency laws, and audit requirements.

General Level Guidance

Level 1 — Introduction

  • Which regulatory frameworks (e.g. GDPR, SOC2, PCI-DSS) must this architecture comply with?
  • What is the overall 'Trust Posture' we present to external auditors or customers?
  • What are the most critical 'Non-Negotiable' compliance items?

Common Fulfillment: A high-level Compliance Statement or a 'Regulatory Landscape' list.

Level 2 — Orientation

  • Where is the primary Evidence Repository for audits located?
  • Who is the Data Protection Officer (DPO) or Compliance Lead?
  • How is data residency managed (e.g. "European data stays in the EU")?

Common Fulfillment: Link to GRC tools (e.g. Vanta, Drata) or an internal Evidence portal.

Level 3 — Elaboration

  • How do specific architectural controls map to individual regulatory requirements (The Control Matrix)?
  • What are the specific technical protocols for 'Data Portability' or 'Right to Erasure'?
  • How are automated compliance checks integrated into the CI/CD pipeline?

Common Fulfillment: A detailed Control Mapping spreadsheet or direct links to automated compliance tests (e.g. OPA, Checkov reports).

Example: Completely over-engineered Todo-App

Level 1 — Introduction

The platform must maintain SOC2 Type II readiness and GDPR compliance to support our B2B SaaS customers who store sensitive corporate task data.

Level 2 — Orientation

We use Vanta as our continuous compliance monitoring tool. Evidence for 'Logical Access' and 'Encryption' is automatically collected from AWS and Auth0. Data residency is strictly enforced: user data is stored in the region where the account was created (either us-east-1 or eu-central-1).

Level 3 — Elaboration

GDPR: Right to Portability Implementation

Users can trigger a Data Export job via the User Service. This job orchestrates a distributed query across the Todo Service and Analytics store, compiles the result into a ZIP of JSON files, and provides a signed S3 link valid for 24 hours.

SOC2 Control: CC6.1 (Access Control)

This control is mapped to our Terraform IAM modules. Every change to an IAM policy must be approved by two individuals and is automatically linted by tfsec to ensure no * wildcard permissions are introduced.
